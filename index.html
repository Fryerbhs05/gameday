      About Conflicted

      Your guy just scored. Now what.

      You run three leagues. Maybe five. The same players keep turning up on both sides of your weekend, and somewhere along the way "who do I root for" stopped being a simple question. Conflicted answers it — every player, every league, in real time.

      Why it exists

      The five-second blank stare

      Daniel Jones breaks a 60-yard touchdown run. Half the room reacts. You don't — not yet — because you can't tell whether you're supposed to. He's your starting QB in the dynasty league, the enemy's desperation streamer in the money league, and riding a bench somewhere you've stopped checking. Good play? You genuinely cannot say. That blank stare, multiplied across every Sunday of the season, is the thing we built Conflicted to end.

      What it does

      The math you can't do in your head

      Conflicted tracks every player across every league you run and keeps a running tally of which side they're on — your guys, the enemy's guys, and the ones who are on both. When a conflicted player does something, we settle the question you'd never settle fast enough on your own: across all of your leagues at once, did that just help you, or hurt you. Then we tell you, in the half-second you actually have to react.

      How it works

      Connect, sort, weigh, verdict

      Connect your leagues — Yahoo, ESPN, Sleeper — and Conflicted pulls your rosters and live matchups. It sorts every player into camps and surfaces the ones with divided loyalty. When one of them scores, we weigh the play across all of your matchups at once: how close each game is, how much football is left to play, and — the part only you can set — which leagues you actually care about.

      Out of that comes one honest read: celebrate, or sulk. The exact formula is ours. What matters is that it reasons the way you would with a spreadsheet open and four minutes per play. You don't have four minutes. That's the point.

      You decide what matters

      Not all leagues are equal

      And we don't pretend they are. You sort your leagues into priority tiers — the championship money league sits above the one you joined out of obligation — and the read bends accordingly. Winning the one that matters can outweigh losing two that don't. That isn't a flaw in how you experience fantasy. It's the entire emotional economy of it, and Conflicted is built around that rather than against it.

      Who it's for

      The over-invested

      Conflicted is not for the person who runs one league with college friends and forgets to set a lineup. It's for the manager three, four, five leagues deep — a decade in, treating Sunday like an unpaid second job, quietly aware of how much head space this rents. We assume you know what RB1 means and have opinions about ADP. We respect that you're too far in to play casually, and we built the tool accordingly.

      What we believe

      A few principles

      • We don't store your league passwords. Connect a platform and we hold a revocable token — nothing more. Cut it loose whenever you want.
      • All your leagues, in one place. Yahoo, ESPN, Sleeper — Conflicted doesn't care where you play. It pulls every league into one view so you're not refreshing four apps at once.
      • It sweats the math so you don't have to. Win probability, league weighting, how much football is left — it runs the read you'd never finish in time and hands you the verdict.
      • It tells you the truth, fast. When a play helps, you'll know. When it hurts, you'll know that too — even when you'd rather not.
      The name

      It's the honest part

      Cheering a player in one league while cursing him in another — at the same moment, in the same five-second window — is a genuinely strange way to spend a fall. Conflicted doesn't fix that; nothing can. It just makes sure that when the strangeness pays off, you know the instant it happens, and when it costs you, you find out fast enough to be appropriately annoyed.
